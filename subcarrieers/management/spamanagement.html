Spa Management

Summary

Spa management is the concept of gaining expertise in spa treatments and knowing how to run a spa. Those who become spa managers are typically passionate about the spa industry. They enjoy working with people, and they are comfortable directing the work of others. Spa managers must have strong leadership skills, and must be willing to take responsibility for the success or failure of the business they operate. They manage the entire activities of a spa ranging from managing the finances to checking the operations.

Career Opportunities in Spa Management

  • General spa manager
    A spa manager takes care of the day-to-day operations of a health or beauty spa. They handle the accounts, maintain the books, manage the employs and the services that are offered by the spa. Depending on the size of the facility, they may be more into the business side of things, or they may play more of a customer service role.
  • Spa designer
    Spa Design is not just about colors, materials, beauty, accessories, etc. It is about creating a structure and an environment which will support the business in all its aspects, including retail sales. Design is an essential tool in generating business and revenues. The most important element in selling retail products in a spa is not the product real value, but its “perceived value
  • Spa marketing manager
    Spa Marketing Coordinator needs to manage marketing needs and front desk duties, maintain professional and impeccable spa environment.

Work Description

  • A spa manager is responsible for the operations of a health or beauty spa.
  • They have to manage employee schedules, staff numbers, customer service, and spa operations.
  • They conduct all of the hiring, firing, and training of employees.
  • They conduct disciplinary actions and performance reviews for employees and sometimes even monitor employees for correct work performance.
  • They have to conduct training workshops for employees, so as to keep their employees up to date with new skills in the spa industry.
  • They may also have to keep a check on inventory sometimes.

Pros & Cons of a Career in Spa Management

Pros
  • A lot of responsibility is attached as you need to coordinate all the work.
  • It involves good remuneration.
  • The job profile gives you an opportunity to travel and meet new people.
Cons
  • It can be a little stressful and emotionally draining at times.
  • It can take a bit of time to build up your business and reputation.
  • You are required to work for long hours.
