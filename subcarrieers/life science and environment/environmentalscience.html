Environmental Science

Summary

Environmental Science is an interdisciplinary field that integrates physical, biological, and information sciences to study the environment and various environmental problems. Environmental Scientists work on prime subjects such as the understanding of earth processes, evaluating alternative energy systems, pollution control and mitigation, natural resource management, and the effects of global climate change.

Career Opportunities in Environmental Science

  • Environmental Analyst
    Environmental Analysts basically help companies public and government understand the impacts of human activities on the environment. They study and analyze the various aspects of the environment to propose policies and practices to make the interactions between the environment and humans less harmful.
  • Environmental Lobbyist
    Environmental Lobbyists work to petition for environmental issues with politicians and government at various levels to influence them to pass laws, regulations and policies for a cleaner and safer environment. They often organize and conduct campaigns to push agendas to promote safer industrial and environmental practices.
  • Environmental Management and Conservation
    Environmental Scientists engaged in the environmental management and conservation sector are interested with the control and resolution of various environmental issues, to conserve the environment. Potential employment opportunities exist with government organizations, private ecological consulting firms, wildlife rehabilitation centers, pollution control board, etc.
  • Environmental consultant
    Environmental scientists can be employed as consultants to provide expert advice and review to government and private organizations on matters of the environment. They also play a significant role in shaping the policies of the government.
  • Waste management officer
    Waste management officers manage and coordinate safe disposal of waste, and conduct recycling activities and related tasks. Opportunities in this domain are available both in the public and private sector.
  • Natural Resource Manager
    A natural resource manager is involved in the handling and general management of plants, lakes, soil, forests, and other natural resources. Jobs for natural resource managers are available in both government and private settings.

Work Description

  • Collecting and examining data from different environments (soil, air and water).
  • Examining the effects of human activities on the environment.
  • Preparing and presenting reports of the information gathered.
  • Developing ways to protect environment from various damages.
  • Executing environmental inspections, investigating transgress.
  • Suggest on the development and conduct of regulatory actions.
  • Estimating and executing environmental plans, strategies and official rules or actions.
  • Developing programmes to protect earth, its environment and habitats.

Pros & Cons of a Career in Environmental Science

Pros
  • Professionals get the opportunities to work on, and lead advanced research projects.
  • Professionals have the opportunity to contribute to imporving the condition of the planet.
  • The field is rapidly growing, and there are a number of research opportunities, both in India and overseas.
  • Professionals can work across various industries such as mines, sewage treatment plants, water treatment plants, fertilizer plants, distilleries etc.
Cons
  • The educational path for the career is long, as a Ph.D is generally preferred.
  • It can be hard to get into the career, as it requires extensive knowledge, skill and traning.
  • The work might require one to work in bad weather and difficult conditions, when conducting fieldwork.
