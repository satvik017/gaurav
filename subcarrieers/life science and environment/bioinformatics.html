Bioinformatics

Summary

Bioinformatics, also referred to as computational biology, is a modern field that combines computer science, statistics, mathematics and engineering with biology. Professionals work on developing tools, techniques and IT systems for analysing and interpreting complex biological data, such as that obtained from biomedical studies. It makes central and globally accessible databases available to scientists, for use in research studies, to analyze data, make comparisons and develop models.

Career Opportunities in Bioinformatics

  • Bioinformatics Analyst
    Bioinformatics analyst carry out research studies in laboratories to develop informatics algorithms and bioinformatics databases which facilitate the understanding and awareness of various diseases and other health conditions.
  • Medical Coding
    Medical coders are involved in the coding of healthcare claims made by patients to obtain claims from insurance companies and government healthcare programs.
  • Pharmaceutical companies
    Bioinformatics researchers and professionals are actively hired by pharmaceutical companies, in research roles and in other departments.
  • Research
    Research is a prime avenue for bioinformatics professionals. Research positions are available at various government agencies, private laboratories, and other organizations.
  • Scientific Writer
    Journalists and writers with a science background also play a major role in informing the general public about relevant and emerging biological issues. Bioinformatics are often employed by high-profile journals as well as online magazines and science blogs or print/media networks.

Work Description

Bioinformatics job is to collect, classify, store and analyze the biochemical and biological information using computers
  • Examining huge molecular datasets for research purposes
  • Presenting and publishing research results and reports
  • Creating and developing innovative software applications or modifying existing applications to meet particular need
  • Developing innovative computational methods and analytical tools according to the need of research
  • Supervising and managing technicians and other bioinformatics staff
  • Creating concise statistics of information concerning human genomes
  • Consulting and coordinating with related departments for product innovation and advancement

Pros & Cons of a Career in Bioinformatics

Pros
  • The work can be mentally stimulating for those who like to deal with logic, and like to solve problems mentally.
  • Professionals get the opportunity to work on, and lead advanced research projects.
  • The field is rapidly growing, and there are a number of research opportunities, both in India and overseas.
  • Competent and trained professionals have the possibility to earn well.
Cons
  • The educational path for the career is long, as a Ph.D. is generally preferred.
  • It can be hard to get into the career, as it requires extensive knowledge, skill and traning.
  • The work can involve long working hours.
  • It involves spending most of the time indoors, in the laboratory.
