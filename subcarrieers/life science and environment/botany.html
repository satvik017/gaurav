Botany

Summary

Botany, also referred to as Plant Biology, involves the study of plants, including their growth, structure, differentiation, evolution and development etc. Botanists study the interactions of plants and the environment, their life processes, observe their growth in different conditions, conduct experiments to see how they convert chemical compounds to complex chemicals, and their genetic properties that control their development. For this end, botanists employ specialized equipment to conduct their researches and prepare reports on their findings.

Career Opportunities in Botany

  • Business and Industries
    Individuals with a degree in Botany are hired by pharmaceuticals, nurseries, horticulturists, and different industries such as food, fermentation, chemical, oil etc.
  • Landscape Industries
    Many Botanists are hired in the landscape industry and find work in the development of parks, botanical gardens, biodiversity parks, aesthetic gardens, etc. Jobs in this area are widely available in the public sector.
  • Natural Resource Centre
    Botanists are actively employed in different natural resource centers, in both the public and private sectors. The position of a Forest Resource Manager is one of the most common and popular ones in this area.
  • Environmental Consultancy
    Botanists can find work with environmental consultancies as biodiversity assessors, restoration experts, environment conversationalists, etc. Such consultancies are present both in the private as well as the public sector.
  • Research
    Research is one the major duties of a botanist. Botanists with various specializations can work in their research area of interest with various government and private organizations, as well as educational institutes.
  • Education
    Educational institutes are one of the leading employers of Botany graduates. Botanists can find jobs in schools, colleges and other educational institutions as a teacher/lecturer.

Work Description

Typical activities can include
  • Recognizing, categorizing, recording and monitoring of plant species and biodiversity
  • Ecological consultancy work, comprising of surveys and environmental impact assessments
  • Managing a botanical collection
  • Hunting for novel species
  • Evaluating the effects of contamination(pollution), or developments such as new buildings, on plant life
  • Identifying and refining chemicals produced by plants for usage in products such as drugs, food, fabrics, solvents, etc.
  • Presenting research results in journals, books and at academic conferences

Pros & Cons of a Career in Botany

Pros
  • Professionals get a chance to work both in clean, indoor laboratory conditions as well as outside and in the greenhouse.
  • There are various career opportunities available in research and various private and public sectors.
Cons
  • The path to a successful career in this field is long, and most research organizations require a qualification of Ph.D.
  • The nature of work can be difficult and involve long working hours.
