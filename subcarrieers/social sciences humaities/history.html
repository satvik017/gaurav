History

Summary

Historians study about the past. Their typical job activities include gathering historical data from numerous sources, holding research on historical information, providing advice or guidance on historical topics, as well as writing reports, articles and books on historical findings and theories. A degree in history supports the development of various significant skills like documentation, argumentation, writing and research.

Career Opportunities in History

  • Research Arena
    After earning an advanced degree in history, one might choose Research Historian as a career. Indian Council of Historian Research ( ICHR) is an autonomous organization that encourages historical research.
  • Government Organizations
    A history degree is also useful in the civil services. One might conduct research to provide historical context for current policy issues or do some other related work. Intelligence Agencies also hire historians to write history of significant moments or events within the agency.
  • Historical Associations
    Historians preserve artifacts and explain the historical significance of a wide variety of subjects, such as historic buildings, battlegrounds, religious groups etc.
  • Education
    After earning a Ph.D degree in history one may become a professor in the college settings or also choose to become a high school teacher or a post-secondary teacher with a B.Ed. degree.
  • Editing and Publishing
    Students of history may find employment in a wide variety of publishing areas, including university presses, textbook and trade houses, magazines and journals, professional associations, museums, and institutional publication offices.
  • Consultants and Contractors
    A career in consulting is ideal for historians who are adventurous or prefer flexibility and a variety of projects. History consultants can perform jobs such as preparing surveying site's, processing an archival collection for a corporation, or researching an exhibit for a museum or court case. Contract historians on the other hand often work on projects in historic preservation, archaeology, architectural history, landscape architecture, and litigation.

Important Facts

  • No mandatory subject in Class XII.
  • Most of the institutes for post-graduation require bachelors in any field or in History or/and related subjects.
  • For Lectureship in History, National Educational Test (NET) or Ph.D. is mandatory
  • Most colleges have eligibility criteria of minimum 50% aggregate marks in Class XII.
  • Most Colleges/Institutes provide admission on the basis of Class 12th Percentage. Some institutes also conduct interviews.
  • There are various other colleges in Delhi University who offer this course.

Work Description

  • Gather historical data from sources such as archives, court records, diaries, news files, and photographs
  • Conduct historical research for the identification, conservation, and reconstruction of historic places and materials
  • Research on and prepare manuscripts in support of public programming and the development of exhibits at historic sites, museums, libraries, and archives
  • Recommend actions related to historical art, such as which items to add to a collection or which items to display in an exhibition
  • Advise individuals and institutions regarding issues such as the historical authenticity of materials or the customs of a specific historical period
  • Interview people to gather information about historical events and to record oral histories
  • Impart and conduct research in colleges, universities, museums, and other research agencies and schools
