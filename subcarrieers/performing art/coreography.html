Choreographer

Summary

A choreographer's work is to create and organize dance sequences in cinema, music videos, stage shows, TV shows, drama, theatre, as well as in private gatherings. They are professionals in dance who create not only dances, but themes too.

Career Opportunities in Choreography

  • Film/ TV
    Choreographers work mostly in movies and TV serials.
  • Shows & Events
    Choreographers help in preparing dance performances for shows and events.
  • Dance Academy
    A choreographer can open his/her own dance academy to share his/her knowledge of dance with others.
  • Dancer
    A choreographer can work as a dancer himself/herself.
  • Dance Teachers
    Choreographers can work as teachers and teach dance to students.

Work Description

  • Invent and develop new dance movements
  • Engage in rehearsals for several hours to maintain flexibility and rhythm
  • Weave a storyline for drama or theatre performance to present through dance
  • Perform at events, gatherings, cultural programmes, etc.
  • Design and develop concepts for dance acts including music, backdrop and costumes
  • Practice regularly for several hours to maintain flexibility
  • Give auditions for various competitions, films and or television
  • Perform as a part of drama and theatre shows
  • Guide the upcoming talents with one's own knowledge and experience
  • Guide couples, dancers and other dance groups in competitions and events

Pros & Cons of a Career as a Choreographer

Pros
  • Helps you boost your self confidence as you perform and instruct others who follow you.
  • It is the best form of work out and thus keeps you fit.
Cons
  • There is a lot of Competetion in this field and it's time consuming.
  • You are always prone to injury. This is a field where the physical pain never goes away
