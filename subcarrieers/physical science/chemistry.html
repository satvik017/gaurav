Chemistry

Summary

Chemistry is a branch of the physical sciences, dealing with the study of the composition, properties, structure, and the changes of matter. It is a part of our everyday lives and studying chemistry helps you develop subject-specific skills valued by all employers and thus opens doors to a wide range of careers, most of which aim to solve global challenges. It is a very versatile field and can help one get into any industrial, educational, scientific or government field.

Career Opportunities in Chemistry

  • Chemical Engineering
    Chemical engineers work in a number of sectors including energy, oil and gas, plastics, water treatment, toiletries, pharmaceuticals food and drink, etc.
  • Pharmaceuticals
    Pharmaceutical chemists design, develop, analyze, and regulate new and existing pharmaceutical drugs. Pharmaceutical chemists research and develop new and cost-effective drugs for the market, and test them to ensure that the products are suitable for public consumption.
  • Healthcare
    Chemists in the healthcare sector are called as Clinical Biochemists. Their analysis takes place in laboratories and healthcare units, and aids the investigation, diagnosis and treatment of diseases.
  • Consultant
    Biochemical Consultants are often sought by government and private organizations for expert review. Legal departments of Corporations, Law firms, Hospitals, etc. also hire consultants.
  • Research
    Even though research scientist work in different settings; most of their work is based in laboratories. Chemical research teams conduct rigorous research by applying scientific methods and standards.
  • Teaching
    Teachers and educators are employed at the primary and senior secondary school level, as well as the college level. Working as a guest lecturer or speaker is also an option.

Work Description

  • Examining samples from different origins to provide data on compounds or amount of compounds present
  • Innovating on techniques to examine drug product and chemicals
  • Using a variety of analytical methods, instruments and software to conduct research on chemicals
  • Examining data and following rules and instructions while writing reports
  • Staying abreast of health and safety matters
  • Coordinating with associated fields and stakeholders
  • Presenting and publishing results based on experiments

Pros & Cons of a Career in Chemistry

Pros
  • There are a number of avenues available for employment, including pharmaceuticals, manufacturing industries, research and development departments of organizations etc.
  • Job can be interesting and intellectually stimulating for those who enjoy analytical work.
  • The pays are high after being established in the industry.
  • Professionals have a chance to provide meaningful contributions to society through discoveries and innovations.
Cons
  • The coursework is difficult, and the pathway to a becoming a successful chemist is long.
  • The nature of the work can be hazardous and can potentially pose a danger to life.
  • Professionals frequently have to work for long hours and under deadlines.
