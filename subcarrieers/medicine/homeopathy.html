Homeopathy

Summary

Homeopathy is the branch of medicine that involves a system of practice that treats disease by the administration of minute doses of a remedy. It was developed by Dr. Samuel Hahnemann, a German physician. It is based on the principle that "like cures like". It implies that any substance, which can produce symptoms in a healthy person, can cure similar symptoms in a person who is sick. It prepares individuals for the independent professional practice of homeopathic medicine, a holistic therapeutic system.

Career Opportunities in Homeopathy

  • Homeopathic Doctor
    Homeopathic Doctors use remedies, including essential oils, herbs and compounds, tinctures, massage, aromatherapy, or even acupuncture. They are often self-employed. Many doctors are able to command higher salaries, though hours may be long and irregular.
  • Homeopathic Medical consultant
    The homeopathic medical consultant gives consultation by taking into consideration the patient's symptoms, personality traits, physical and psychological state and also the life history.
  • Homeopathic Professor/Lecturer/Reader
    Homeopathic Professor/Lecturer/Reader teach in government and private homeopathic colleges.
  • Homeopathy pharmacist-cum-dispenser
    Homeopathy pharmacist-cum-dispensers sell homeopathic medicines.
  • Hospital Manager
    They work with all the individuals involved in the administration to plan, strategize and coordinate the health services of a hospital. They supervise all the areas of a hospital which includes the physicians, health information technicians, nursing, medical records etc.
  • Chief Medical Officer
    The workday of the Chief Medical officer includes treating patients as well as administrative work. They cooperate with the executive team and initiate procedures. Their work also includes training, motivating and supporting of the staff.

Pros & Cons of a Career in Homeopathy

Pros
  • Being able to help others: Homeopathic doctors provide a meaningful service using medical science for others. Many individuals are drawn to the profession because they want to help others.Homeopathy mediciences are safe to consume with less side effects
  • Job security: This is one profession which would always be in demand and is not affected by any recession. We all will always need doctors.
  • Money and security:
    • Doctors have a relatively high salary and income. Their demand is on increasing rate.
  • Prestige: They generally gain community respect.
Cons
  • Long training route: To practice as a doctor it requires at least four years of college with internship and 2-3 years of masters in the specialized area. It makes the whole process too long before the person actually starts to practice.
  • High college fees: The fees of medical colleges is generally very high.
  • Long work hours, high stress and patience: The job is very demanding. It requires physical, mental and emotional involvement and drains his/her energy. The homeopathic doctor should be very patient and understanding.
