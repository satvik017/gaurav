Civil Engineering

Summary

Civil Engineers plan, design, construct and manage construction projects. The projects span from various domains including transportation, water supply and waste management, estate planning and even taking up offshore projects. Civil Engineers assume two roles, that of a consultant and a contractor. A consultant works with the client to plan, design and manage the projects. A contractor supervises the construction and manages the project on ground level to turn plans into realities.

Career Opportunities in Civil Engineering

  • Structural engineering
    Civil Engineers in this field create structural elements of design for bridges, skyscrapers, ports and airports etc. They also design and assess major projects, such as buildings, bridges, or dams, to ensure their strength and durability.
  • Geotechnical engineering
    Civil Engineers in this field work exclusively with rock and soil that support the structures and make sure that the foundations are solid. They focus on how structures built by civil engineers, such as buildings and tunnels, interact with the earth (including soil and rock). In addition, they design and plan for slopes, retaining walls, and tunnels.
  • Environmental engineering
    Civil Engineers in this field design systems and structures for treatment of chemical, biological and thermal wastes.
  • Materials engineering
    Civil Engineers in this field deal with the inspection of existing and development of new materials which can be used for construction projects.
  • Maintenance
    Civil Engineers in this field ensure that the structures built, are looked after properly and all the necessary repairs and renovations take place.
  • Water resource engineering
    Civil Engineers in this field focus on collection, storage and management of water.
  • Earthquake engineering
    Civil Engineers specializing in this field create buildings and other structures that can withstand the stresses of earthquakes without falling.
  • Coastal engineering
    Civil Engineers specializing in this field manage the coastal areas, including marshlands. They create defenses against flooding and erosion.

Work Description

  • Create graphical representations of civil structures
  • Inspect project sites to monitor progress and ensure compliance with design specifications and safety standards
  • Provide technical advice to industrial or managerial personnel regarding design, construction, or structural repairs
  • Estimate quantities and cost of materials, equipment, or labour cost to determine project feasibility
  • Test building materials, such as concrete, asphalt, or steel, for use in particular projects
  • Consider construction costs, government regulations, potential environmental hazards and other factors in planning stages and carry out risk analysis
  • Compile and submit permit applications to local, state, and federal agencies verifying that projects comply with various regulations
  • Present findings to public on topics such as bid proposals or environmental impact reports

Pros & Cons of a Career in Civil Engineering

Pros
  • The demand for civil engineers always remains high, so does the pay package.
  • They are major figures involved in the development of community.
  • Opportunity to travel to different locations and work on different sites.
Cons
  • It is a high-pressure job.
  • There are no fixed work hours, and they spend much time on construction sites and remote locations.
  • Scrutiny of quality of work and material from third parties.
