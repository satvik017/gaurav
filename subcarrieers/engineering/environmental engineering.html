Environmental Engineering

Summary

Environmental Engineers evaluate, study and manage the detrimental effects of human activity on the environment. They find solutions for issues concerning pollution and public health. Their work primarily revolves around three areas - waste management and disposal, reclamation of degraded land and control of pollutants. They also study the impact of technology advancements on environment. With the increasing focus of international and national bodies/ government towards environment conservation, the future prospects for environmental engineers are extremely bright.

Career Opportunities in Environmental Engineering

  • Agricultural Engineers
    An agricultural engineer makes farming sustainable, safe, and environmentally friendly. They analyze agricultural operations and weigh the use of new technologies and methods to increase yields, improve land use, and conserve resources like seed, water, fertilizers, pesticides and fuel.
  • Biologists
    Biologists study biotic forms like plants and animals and the world they live in. It is a good field for people interested in the natural world and who enjoy practical science.
  • Chemical Engineers
    Chemical Engineers work principally in the chemical industry to convert basic raw materials into a variety of products.
  • Analytical Chemists
    Analytical chemists use a diverse range of methods to investigate the chemical nature of substances. The aim of such work is to identify and understand substances and how they behave in different conditions.
  • Recycling Plants
    Environmental Engineers develop technologies to recycle waste material and ensure their proper utilization.
  • Ecologists
    Ecologists are concerned with ecosystems as a whole, the abundance and distribution of organisms (people, plants, animals), and the relationships between organisms and their environment.
  • Legal Consultants
    Environmental Engineers often work as advisors to legal firms dealing with issues and companies involved in environmental degradation.
  • Conservationist
    Environmental Engineers work towards innovating processes related to conservation of air, soil and water. They work with Government, NGOs as well as multinationals engaged in this act.

  • Most college have eligibility criteria of minimum 50% aggregate marks in Class XII / and some of minimum 60%.
  • Must have passed class 12th or senior secondary examination with at least 50% marks including English.
  • Some Exams require JEE score as minimum eligibility.
  • Some colleges/ universities also have an age limit.

Work Description

  • Supervise the design of systems, processes, or equipments for control, management, or remediation of water, air, or soil quality.
  • Collaborate with environmental scientists, planners, hazardous waste technicians, engineers, or other specialists to address environmental problems.
  • Provide technical support for environmental remediation of projects, including remediation system designing or determination of regulatory applicability.
  • Prepare hazardous waste manifests or land disposal restriction notifications.
  • Assess the existing or potential environmental impact of projects on air, water, or land.
  • Prepare, maintain, or revise quality assurance documentation or procedures.
  • Assist in budget implementation, forecasts, or administration.
  • Provide environmental engineering assistance in network analysis, regulatory analysis, or planning or reviewing database development.

Pros & Cons of a Career in Environmental Engineering

Pros
  • Can work in a number of settings. No need to be restricted to Office.
  • Most work is to be done in a team, which is less burdening.
  • Satisfaction of helping the environment.
Cons
  • Having to deal with deadlines can be stressful.
  • Working in polluted or hazardous environment much of the time.
  • Salary will only increase with experience and hard-work.
