Sound Engineering

Summary

A Sound engineering technician operates, assembles and maintains technical equipment that records, amplifies, enhances, mixes or reproduces sounds. Sound engineers work in pan domains like advertising, music, movies, live performances and broadcasting. They assess the requirement of sound at any given location and then produce the appropriate decibels of sound.

Career Opportunities in Sound Engineering

  • Recording
    Sound Engineers in this profile record acoustics/design across field recording, sound libraries, bio acoustic research; nature sounds, live performances, mixing engineering, music composition and music production.
  • Internet audio broadcast
    Sound Engineers in this profile work across Internet, Radio, Television, Web Casting.
  • Mastering CD + DVD live sound
    Sound Engineers in this profile, master their hand at recording live sound in CD's and DVD's.
  • Audio software + hardware
    Sound Engineers in this profile work in quality assurance and technical support.
  • Quality Assessment
    Sound Engineers check the quality of sound and modify it to make it more pleasing.
  • Music restoration/preservation
    Sound Engineers work here to preserve prerecorded materials, such as vintage LP's and cassettes.
  • Forensic audio
    Sound engineers specializing in this profile work in courtroom documentation and as experts for speech recognition /restoration.

Work Description

  • Consult and advise producers, directors and performers to identify the sound requirements
  • Apply technical knowledge & experience in the domain of sound recording to help clients achieve the desired artistic objectives
  • Evaluate the audibility of region and also manage the overall assembly of the required equipments
  • Integrate and synchronize audio effects with visual content
  • Create sound effects which are used in shows, movies, broadcasting and television
  • Mix and balance music, special effects, speech and video graphics

Pros & Cons of a Career in Sound Engineering

Pros
  • Will find opportunities to work with TV and Film Personalities, editors, directors, singers
  • Combines passion for music and engineering
  • Great prospects in the near future
Cons
  • Need additional certification and experience to move ahead
  • Can be confused with jobs like DJing and Music Composer
  • May not make enough money at initial stages
