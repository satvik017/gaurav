Mining Engineering

Summary

Mining engineering examines the viability, safety and output of potential mines. Mining engineers ensure safe and efficient development of mines and optimize extraction of ore reserves. They also carry out research and develop equipment that reduce the environmental impact of mining and closely work with geotech, surveying and metallurgy experts.

Career Opportunities in Mining Engineering

  • Consultant
    Consultants conduct sub-surface surveys to identify the characteristics of potential mining development sites. They specify the ground support systems, processes and equipment for safe, economical, and environmentally sound extraction or underground construction activities. They also inspect areas for unsafe geological conditions, equipment, and working conditions.
  • Production Manager
    Mine managers are responsible for planning, organizing and supervising the activities of a mine. They are responsible for planning future mine production, overseeing the development and tunneling of the mine and inspecting the mine for danger. Mine managers also carry out generic management duties, such as hiring staff and liaising and negotiating with suppliers, contractors and other stakeholders.
  • Operations Manager
    Operations managers oversee project engineering activities during the construction phase of the mine. They are responsible for facility planning, process engineering, maintenance and application of automation and advanced technology and engineering work relating to project development.
  • Health and Safety Manger Analyst
    Health & Safety Analysts support the Health & Safety department in the development and implementation of the Health and Safety Management System (HSMS), Emergency Management Program, and Security Plan.
  • Mine Planning
    Mine Planners examine mining methods and equipment to improve performance, prepare drilling and blasting strategies, develop production strategies & assist with estimating mine plan costs and economic evaluations.
  • Mine Ventilation engineer
    A mine ventilation engineer ensures a steady supply of fresh & clean air for miners to work in. This means controlling heat and humidity levels that quickly can become overwhelming inside mines and removing noxious gases that may come from the Earth itself as well as the carbon dioxide generated by miners.
  • Mining Investment Analyst
    Mining Investment Analysts develop and maintain financial models for valuation and other purposes. They review and analyze financial statements of private and public companies & also assist senior members of metals & mining team with tracking and analyzing minerals and metals commodity markets.
  • Petroleum Engineer
    Petroleum engineers are involved in nearly all of the stages of oil and gas field evaluation, development and production. Their aim is to maximize hydrocarbon recovery at minimum cost, while maintaining a strong emphasis on reducing environmental impact.

Work Description

  • Design, implement, and monitor the development of mines, facilities, systems, or equipments.
  • Examine maps, deposits, drilling locations, or mines to determine the location, size, accessibility,value and potential profitability of mineral, oil, and gas deposits.
  • Devise solutions to problems of land reclamation, water and air pollution, such as methods of storing excavated soil and turning exhausted mine sites to natural state.
  • Select or devise material handling methods and equipment to transport ore, waste materials, and mineral products efficiently and economically.
  • Test air quality to detect toxic gases and recommend measures to remove them, such as installation of ventilation shafts.
  • Conduct or direct mining operations to test or prove test findings.
  • Assess the commercial viability of new mining ventures
