Biomedical and Biotechnology Engineering

Summary

Biomedical/Biotech Engineers research, design and develop medical products and apply the principles of engineering and material technology to healthcare. They design and implement new technologies like computer facilitated operations and tissue engineering. They work closely with physicians, surgeons, life science experts and chemists to develop engineering facets of biological systems.

Career Opportunities in Biomedical and Biotechnology Engineering

  • Corporate Firms/ Pharmaceutical companies
    Biotechnologists & Biomedical Engineers create products that require an in-depth understanding of living systems and technology. They work in research and development or in quality assurance.
  • Research
    Biotechnologists & Biomedical Engineers work with medical scientists, doctors and chemists to conduct research to solve clinical problems.
  • Hospitals
    Biotechnologists & Biomedical Engineers design softwares to run medical equipment, or simulations to test drug therapies. Design and build artificial body parts or replacement body parts.

How to Pursue a Career in Biomedical and Biotechnology Engineering

Stream Graduation After Graduation After Post Graduation
Path 1
 Clear Class XII with Science Stream with Maths Pursue B.Tech in Biomedical Engineering/ Biotechnology for 4 years Pursue M.Tech for 2-3 years Pursue Ph.D for 2-3 years
Path 2
 Clear Class XII with Science Stream with Maths Pursue B.Tech + M.Tech (Dual Degree)in Biomedical Engineering/ Biotechnology for 5 years - Pursue Ph.D for 2-3 years

Important Facts

  • Most colleges have eligibility criteria of minimum 50% aggregate marks in Class XII / and some of minimum 60%.
  • Must have passed class 12thor senior secondary examination with at least 50% marks including English.
  • Some colleges/ universities also have an age limit.
  • Biomedical Engineering is available as a course only in select colleges and it is different from Biomedical Science.

Work Description

  • Conduct research, along with life scientists, chemists, and medical scientists, on the engineering aspects of the biological systems of the humans and animals.
  • Design and develop medical diagnostic and clinical instrumentation, equipments, and procedures, using the principles of engineering and bio-behavioral sciences.
  • Develop models or computer simulations of human bio-behavioural systems to obtain data for measuring or controlling life processes.
  • Write documents describing protocols, policies, standards for use, maintenance and repair of medical equipments.
  • Advise hospital administrators on the planning, acquisition and use of medical equipments.
  • Conduct training to educate clinicians and other personnel on proper use of equipments.
  • Develop new applications for energy sources, such as using nuclear power for biomedical implants.

Pros & Cons of a Career in Biomedical and Biotechnology Engineering

Pros
  • Opportunity to pursue new research in new fields like bioinformatics
  • Chance to improve existing technologies
  • Practical field, where application oriented minds can work
Cons
  • Limited number of jobs as of now
  • Limited seats and colleges offering the course
  • May not be highly rewarding in terms of money
