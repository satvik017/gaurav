Statistics

Summary

Statistics is a field concerned with the collection, analysis, interpretation and presention of numerical data. Statisticians use data to solve problems for different organisations. A student who pursues statistics learns about both pure statistics like probability theory, statistical methodology, stochastic processes, statistical inference, operational research, bio statistics, etc., and also the application of these statistical concepts. Statisticians are uniquely qualified to help both the public and private in order to prepare, organize and analyse their respective data.

Career Opportunities in Statistics

  • Public sector
    Government organizations actively hire statisticians for various functions, such as assessing population demographics and characteristics, economic measurements and policy formulation. These organizations mostly include statistical and economic bureaus, and those related to socio-economic research. Other than this, banks, environmental, scientific and agricultural agencies also employ statisticians for similar type of work in their respective fields.
  • Private sector
    In the private sector a statistician can become part of large multi-national companies. Banks and insurance companies need statisticans who have the knowledge of blending both maths and stats to create different financial models /products (eg. loans, investments etc.). Statisticians are also hired for varied roles, such as project development, reserch operations, commercial surveys and numerical analysis in share markets, stock markets etc.
  • Healthcare Sector
    Statisticians also known as biostatisticians/biometricians are employed in hospitals, pharmaceutical companies or public health agencies. Their work includes designing studies to test whether drugs successfully treat diseases or conditions. They may also assist hospitals or public health agencies in identifying the origins of outbreaks of illnesses in humans and animals.
  • Education
    A prime avenue for statistics graduates is the education sector. They can work as full-time or guest lecturers at universities and colleges. They may also teach in schools to higher secondary class students.
  • Research
    A large number of mathematicians end up in research roles. The indispensability of mathematics demands the employment of mathematicians in various research organizations to solve complex problems. They are even engaged by aeronautical research agencies such as Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and National Aeronautic Limited (NAL).

Work Description

Statistician's job is to plan and conduct the trials and inspections, and deal with the first collection of data. They operate and examine the data in various situations, finding patterns to help draw conclusions.
Typical work activities include
  • Discussing with clients and agreeing what and how to gather data, considering any legal and moral parameters
  • Designing experiments, inspections to develop the needed data
  • Gathering and examining the data collated
  • Apply statistical tools and techniques to solve business problems
  • Analyzing and compiling data and ensuring that the correct conclusions are drawn based on the outcomes
  • Presenting results to senior members/ clients
  • Recommending policy-makers on important decisions based on results drawn

Pros & Cons of a Career in Statistics

Pros
  • Professionals get the opportunities to work on, and lead advanced research projects.
  • The field is rapidly growing, and there are a number of opportunities, both in India and overseas.
  • Professionals can find emploment in several roles and sectors such as finance, banks, market research, product testing, etc.
  • The field has high earning and growth potential.
Cons
  • The educational path for the career is long, and a Ph.D is generally preferred for research roles.
  • It involves spending most of the time indoors, with numbers.
  • It can be hard to get into the career, as it requires extensive knowledge, skill and traning.
  • The field is highly competitive and difficult to get into.
