Mathematics

Summary

Mathematics is a field of study concerned with topics such as numbers, space, time, equations, fractions, geometrical shapes, and their relationships. Mathematicians utilize their extensive knowledge of mathematics to observe the relationships between mathematical principles and create mathematical models, to solve real world problems.

Career Opportunities in Mathematics

  • Private Sector
    Mathematics professionals can be employed by MNCs and private organizations to apply their mathematical knowledge in several roles. They can work in data analysis, project development, reserch operations, numerical analysis and commercial surveys for share markets, etc. Banking, insurance, and finance sectors are some of the leading recruiters.
  • Public Sector
    Mathematicians are also hired in government organizations and PSUs for roles such as research operations, commercial surveys and numerical analysis.
  • Education
    A prime avenue for mathematics graduates is the education sector. They can work as full-time or guest lecturers at universities and colleges. They may also teach in schools to higher secondary class students.
  • Defence
    Mathematicians are also actively employed in defence research organizations, such as the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), as well as other military and para-military organizations, to help in the development of a wide range of weapons, missiles, nuclear stations,etc
  • Research
    A large number of mathematicians end up in research roles. The indispensability of mathematics demands the employment of mathematicians in various research organizations to solve complex problems. They are even engaged by aeronautical research agencies such as Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and National Aeronautic Limited (NAL).

Pros & Cons of a Career in Mathematics

Pros
  • Professionals get the opportunities to work on, and lead advanced research projects.
  • The field is rapidly growing, and there are a number of research opportunities, both in India and overseas.
  • Professionals can find emploment in several other fields, such as finance, market research, software development, etc.
  • The field has high earning and growth potential.
Cons
  • The educational path for the career is long, and a Ph.D is generally preferred for research roles.
  • It involves spending most of the time indoors, with numbers.
  • It can be hard to get into the career, as it requires extensive knowledge, skill and traning.
  • The field is highly competitive and difficult to get into.
