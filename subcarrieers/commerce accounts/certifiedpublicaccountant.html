Certified Public Accountant

Summary

Certified Public Accountant or CPA provides accounting services to the public and organizations. They are primarily involved in auditing and attesting procedures. They are very well versed with the accounting principles, business and financial concepts, law, tax, etc. CPAs are hired by organizations in the role of finance manager, financial analyst, chief financial officer, etc. There is an examination which is conducted by American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) and is called as Uniform Certified Public Accountant Examination. In order to practice as a CPA, one need to pass the examination conducted by AICPA. The CPAs are hired by various consulting firms, auditing firms, they can provide service in any area of finance broadly in corporate governance, financial accounting, estate planning etc.

Career Opportunities as a Certified Public Accountant

  • Public Accounting
    CPAs are required in the accounting domain and managing the finances of the public. They are hired by private and government organizations for advisory roles. There are various CPA accounting firms and the CPAs obtain the role of staff auditor wherein they are involved in detailed financial work, tax staff wherein they handle the spheres of tax, management services, etc.
  • Management Accounting
    As a CPA, you need to prepare budgets and financial documents, evaluate and refine accounting procedures, and consult with management teams to help them make good business decisions.
  • Financial Analysts
    As a financial analyst, you have to support the growth of the business by analyzing financial data, preparing reports to illustrate technical information, interpreting data affecting investment programs and helping management make informed decisions.
  • Government Sector
    CPAs in Government have responsibilities in the areas of auditing, financial reporting and management accounting. They have the power to work with the policies which are made at various levels of Government. CPAs provide the information as in how the finances can be used in the best possible manner.
  • Corporate Accounting
    CPAs are hired in corporates for handling Accounts wherein they handle the accounting, auditing and the finances. They manage the pay rolls, property and financial statements. They are considered as the strategic business partners of the organization. They also work as Internal Auditors which contribute to companyâ€™s operations by examining risk management issues, financial data and operations, and control processes.
  • Tax Accounting
    As CPA, you have to maintain tax records and complete tax returns to guide companies in tax strategies, compliance, and tax laws and regulations.
  • Non-Profit Organizations
    CPAs are also employed in the non profit organizations as they help NPOs to manage their revenue, understand the best practices of managing financial systems and taxes. CPAs are hired by NGOs or they can work as a freelancer wherein they can work with multiple organizations.

Work Description

  • The job of CPA involves maintaining and auditing the financial records of the public or organizations.
  • CPAs also come up with strategic ways for budgeting and using the money in the most efficient way possible.
  • They act as a consultant in the areas of accounts, finances, budget, etc.
  • CPAs also attend to tax related work wherein they make sure that the taxes are paid on time by analysing financial information properly.
  • It is a job of responsibility as they also have to report any form of fraudulent work, tax related issue or any other financial issue to the organization.
