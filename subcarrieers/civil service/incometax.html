Income Tax

Summary

A career with Income Tax Department (Government of India) gives an opportunity to work in one of the most important function of the government revenue generation setup. A knack for people as well as for numbers is very important to succeed in this department. The various services in the department provide a wonderful opportunity for a stable career along with the chance in contributing to the nation's development.

Career Opportunities in Income Tax

  • Prosecutor
    A Prosecutor is a lawyer specializing in economic offenses, he/she is hired to represent the department in court cases involving the department and any other party.
  • Tax Assistant
    A Tax Assistant calculates and assess individuals total liable tax incurred on their income and the groups they are catogrized in. He/ She needs to be good with number with an attitude to help others to be able to do their job successfully.
  • Inspector
    An Income Tax Inspector handles and manages various subordinates under him/her. They have the power to search and seize a person/ goods if found suspicious.
  • Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax
    An Assistant Commissioner also known as area manager. He/ she supervises inspectors and other subordinates in the task of collection and vigilance.

Work Description

  • Ensure that the government receives tax from businesses and citizens
  • Maintain accounts of tax from different departments
  • Send notice and conduct periodic checks for frauds and other illegal activities
  • Assist people in tax related problems and queries
  • Investigate into claims of inability to pay taxes by researching court information for the status of liens, mortgages, or financial statements, or by locating assets through third parties
  • Examine and analyze tax assets and liabilities to determine resolution of delinquent tax problems.
  • Communicate with companies, lawyers, colleagues and accountants

Pros & Cons of a Career in Income Tax

Pros
  • Job security and a stable income.
  • Assured pension, medical and other benefits.
  • Less amount of work load.
  • Better pay at low and middle levels (as a result of the 7thpay commission).
Cons
  • Transferrable nature of jobs
  • Technologically backward.
  • Retirement age is fixed.
  • Working hours can be irregular with uncertain duration.
